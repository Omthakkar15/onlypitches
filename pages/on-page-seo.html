On-Page SEO — The Checklist That Actually Gets You Found on Google

You built a website. Now Google needs to understand it. Here's every on-page element that matters — written for business owners in India, not SEO agencies.

India · 2026 Guide

What is On-Page SEO?

On-page SEO is everything you control inside your website that tells Google what your page is about. Think of it like labelling boxes before a move — if the label says "kitchen stuff," the movers know where to put it. Google works the same way.

Unlike off-page SEO (backlinks, social signals), on-page SEO is 100% in your hands. And for a local business in Mumbai, Delhi, Ahmedabad, or anywhere in India — it's often the fastest win you'll get.

Complete On-Page SEO Checklist

Work through this list page by page. You don't need to nail everything on day one — but every item you skip is a ranking signal you're leaving on the table.

  • Content Quality — Write for humans first. Answer real questions your customers ask. Thin, copy-pasted content won't rank in 2026.
  • Meta Title & Description — Your SERP headline and snippet. Keep titles under 60 characters, descriptions under 155. Include your city or "India" where natural.
  • URL Optimisation — Short, readable URLs like /pages/why-website-for-business.html beat messy ones with IDs and parameters.
  • Heading Tags (H1–H6) — One H1 per page. Use H2s for sections, H3s for sub-points. Bold and italic where it helps readability — not for stuffing keywords.
  • Canonical Tag — Tells Google which URL is the "original" when duplicate content exists. Prevents your own pages from competing against each other.
  • Internal & External Links — Link between your own pages (internal). Link to trusted sources (external). Both build context and trust.
  • Image Optimisation — Compress images, use descriptive alt text, and pick WebP format where possible. A 4MB hero image kills your speed score.
  • XML Sitemap — A roadmap for Google crawlers. Submit it via Google Search Console. We include one at sitemap.xml.
  • Robots.txt — Tells crawlers what to index and what to skip. Ours lives at robots.txt.
  • 301 Redirections — When you change a URL, redirect the old one. Otherwise you lose all the SEO equity that page built up.
  • Custom 404 Page — A friendly "page not found" with links back home. Better than a blank error that sends visitors away forever.
  • Schema Markup (JSON-LD) — Structured data that helps Google show rich results — star ratings, FAQs, business hours, and more.
  • Open Graph & Twitter Cards — Controls how your link looks when shared on WhatsApp, Facebook, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X.
  • Page Speed — Google measures Core Web Vitals. Compress assets, lazy-load images, and avoid bloated scripts.
  • Mobile Friendliness — Over 80% of Indian internet users are on mobile. If your site breaks on a phone, Google notices.
  • Fix Broken Links — Run a monthly check. Dead links erode trust with both users and search engines.

Meta Tags — Your First Impression on Google

When someone searches "best salon website India" or "restaurant website builder", your meta title is the blue link they click. Your meta description is the grey text below it.

Pro tip from the trenches: Write your meta description like a WhatsApp forward you'd actually send to a friend — clear, specific, and with a reason to click. "Premium website templates for 55+ Indian business types. Free to browse. Chat with us on WhatsApp." beats "Welcome to our website homepage."

Heading Structure — Don't Skip the Hierarchy

Every page needs exactly one H1 — your main topic. Then break content into H2 sections. Use italic for emphasis on key phrases and bold for terms that matter to your reader.

Google doesn't care about bold vs italic for ranking directly — but well-structured headings improve readability, which keeps people on your page longer. And dwell time? That's a signal.

Quick wins for Indian businesses this week

  1. Add a unique meta title and description to your homepage
  2. Submit your sitemap in Google Search Console
  3. Check mobile view on your phone — fix anything that clips or overflows
  4. Add alt text to every image on your site
  5. Link your new pages together (internal linking)
