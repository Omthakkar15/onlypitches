How to Build a Website for Your Business — India Guide

No fluff, no ₹2 lakh agency quotes. Just the real steps to get your business online — DIY or with a local developer.

India · Step-by-Step

Before you build — know what you're building

Learning how to build a website for your business starts with one question: what should the site actually do? A restaurant needs a menu and booking form. A clinic needs service pages and appointments. A coaching centre needs course listings.

Write down three things: who your customer is, what they need from you, and what action you want (call, book, buy, visit). Everything else follows from that.

Option 2: Hire a local web developer

Search for a website developer near me on Google and you'll find freelancers and agencies in Ahmedabad, Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi, and every major city. A good local developer understands your market and can customise beyond what templates offer.

Ask for portfolio work, get a written quote, and confirm what's included: design, development, domain, hosting, and support. A fair 5–8 page business site in India typically costs ₹15,000–₹50,000.

Look for a best website developer who's built sites in your industry — not just someone who codes well but doesn't understand what a salon or clinic homepage actually needs.

Option 3: Website builder platforms

Wix, WordPress, and similar drag-and-drop tools give control without code. WordPress powers 43% of the internet. The trade-off: you manage updates, security, and hosting yourself.

Our recommendation: Start with a premium template to get online fast. Once you're earning, hire a developer to customise advanced features and SEO. Don't spend ₹80,000 before validating that being online helps your business.

The build process — step by step

  1. Choose your domain — Short, easy to spell, close to your business name. .in or .com both work in India.
  2. Pick your design — Match your industry. A law firm shouldn't look like a nightclub.
  3. Write your content — Homepage headline, about, services, contact. Write like you talk.
  4. Add real photos — Your own images beat stock photos. Phone photos work if well-lit.
  5. Set up contact methods — WhatsApp button, form, phone, Google Maps. Make reaching you effortless.
  6. Optimise for Google — Meta titles, descriptions, headings, alt text. Use our SEO optimisation checklist.
  7. Test on mobile — Tap every button on your phone. Fix annoyances before launch.
  8. Go live — Connect domain, submit sitemap to Search Console, announce on WhatsApp.

What to budget — real numbers for India

  • Domain: ₹500–₹1,200 per year
  • Hosting: ₹2,000–₹6,000 per year
  • DIY template: free to ₹5,000 one-time
  • Freelance developer: ₹15,000–₹50,000
  • Agency: ₹50,000–₹2,00,000+

The sweet spot for most Indian small businesses is ₹10,000–₹30,000. That gets you a genuinely good site with the right developer or platform. A website developer under budget doesn't mean cheap quality — it means smart choices.

After launch — don't ghost your website

  • Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
  • Create a Google Business Profile — free and powerful for local search in India
  • Share your link on WhatsApp status, Instagram bio, and visiting card
  • Update content every few months
  • Test load speed on 4G — most of India browses on mobile data
