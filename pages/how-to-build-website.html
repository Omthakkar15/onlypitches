How to Build a Website for Your Business — A Practical India Guide

No fluff, no ₹2 lakh agency quotes. Just the real steps to get your business online — whether you DIY it or hire someone local.

India · Step-by-Step

Before you build — know what you're building

Most website projects fail before they start — not because of bad design, but because nobody asked "what should this website actually do?" A restaurant needs a menu and reservation form. A clinic needs service pages and appointment booking. A coaching centre needs course listings.

Write down three things: who your customer is, what they need from you, and what action you want them to take (call, book, buy, visit). Everything else follows from that.

Three ways to get your website built

Option 1: DIY with premium templates — fastest & most affordable

Pick a ready-made template for your industry, swap in your logo, colours, photos, and text. Platforms like BizBuilder offer 55+ business-specific designs — hotel, salon, clinic, restaurant, gym, and more. You can have something live in a day, not a month.

Best for: Small businesses, startups, and anyone who wants a polished site without a massive budget. This is honestly the best website under budget route for most Indian SMBs.

Option 2: Hire a local web developer

If you search for a website developer near me on Google, you'll find dozens of freelancers and agencies in your city — Ahmedabad, Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi, wherever you are. A good local developer understands your market, speaks your language, and can customise things templates can't.

Ask for their portfolio, get a written quote, and clarify what's included: design, development, domain, hosting, and ongoing support. A fair project for a 5–8 page business site in India typically runs ₹15,000–₹50,000 depending on complexity.

When comparing developers, look for someone who's built sites in your industry — not just someone who codes beautifully but has no idea what a salon or clinic actually needs on their homepage.

Option 3: Website builder platforms (Wix, WordPress, etc.)

Drag-and-drop builders give you control without code. WordPress powers about 43% of the internet and offers endless plugins. The trade-off? You're responsible for updates, security, and hosting. Fine if you're tech-comfortable; overwhelming if you're not.

Our honest recommendation: Start with a premium template to get online fast. Once you're earning from the site, invest in a best website developer to customise the parts that matter most — custom features, advanced SEO, integrations. Don't spend ₹80,000 before you've validated that your business benefits from being online.

The build process — step by step

  1. Choose your domain — Keep it short, easy to spell, and close to your business name. yourbusinessname.in or .com both work well in India. Register via GoDaddy, Namecheap, or BigRock.
  2. Pick your design — Match your industry. A law firm shouldn't look like a nightclub. Browse templates by business type and pick one that feels like your brand.
  3. Write your content — Homepage headline, about section, services/products, contact info. Write like you talk. Skip the corporate jargon.
  4. Add photos — Real photos of your business beat stock images every time. Phone photos are fine if they're well-lit and clear.
  5. Set up contact methods — WhatsApp button, contact form, phone number, Google Maps embed. Make it stupid-easy to reach you.
  6. Optimise for Google — Meta titles, descriptions, heading structure, alt text on images. Follow our on-page SEO checklist.
  7. Test on mobile — Open your site on your phone. Tap every button. Fill every form. If it's annoying on mobile, fix it before launch.
  8. Connect your domain & go live — Point your domain to your hosting, submit your sitemap to Google Search Console, and announce it.

What should you budget? Real numbers for India

Let's talk money, because nobody does:

  • Domain: ₹500–₹1,200/year
  • Hosting: ₹2,000–₹6,000/year (shared hosting is fine to start)
  • Template / DIY: Free to ₹5,000 one-time
  • Freelance developer: ₹15,000–₹50,000 for a standard business site
  • Agency: ₹50,000–₹2,00,000+ depending on scope

If someone quotes you ₹3,000 for a "full custom website," be skeptical. If someone quotes ₹5 lakh for a 5-page site, also be skeptical. The sweet spot for most Indian small businesses is ₹10,000–₹30,000 — and yes, that gets you a genuinely good site if you pick the right person or platform.

After launch — don't ghost your website

Building the site is half the job. After going live:

  • Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console
  • Create a Google Business Profile (free, massive for local search)
  • Share your site link on WhatsApp status, Instagram bio, and visiting card
  • Update content every few months — fresh content signals an active business
  • Check your site loads fast on a 4G connection (most of India still browses on mobile data)
